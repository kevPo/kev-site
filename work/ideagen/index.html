Qualtrax

Even while working at Excella, I remained in close contact with my friends from Qualtrax. As great as Excella was, there was a part of me that missed working on a single product with a small team. After a few conversations I made the difficult choice to go back to Qualtrax, only this time I would be primarily focusing on our backend code.

Not long after my return, I became the team lead for a team of three engineers called the Ghostbusters. We were mostly responsible for fixing emergency issues while refactoring our massive legacy product.

Ideagen acquired Qualtrax just a little bit before I left. It was a bittersweet moment for sure. Sadly not many of the same folks work there today.

Team: Ghostbusters

Team Lead

  • Team transitioned from 4-5 yearly releases to an automated weekly release via Azure Pipelines.
  • Team reduced user story lead-time from 73 days to 17 within 1 year using Agile and DevOps principles.
  • Owner of the collaboration between customer support and product engineering. Responsible for facilitating all escalations and critical failures.
  • Organized and implemented major initiative to remove all XSS vulnerabilities from a large WebForms product.
